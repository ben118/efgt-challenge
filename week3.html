Overview

You have made it to Week Three! By now, hopefully you are feeling all of the positive results of your hard work during the past couple of weeks. Sometime during this week, try to make time to take a look back on your journey and notice all of the obstacles that you have overcome. Are you feeling proud? You should be!

This week will be about redesigning your life so that you can create long­term healthy habits. If you want to get healthy and lose weight long term, your sleep needs to be one of your major priorities. In fact, besides eating whole foods and moving your body, getting enough sleep is the most important thing you can do for your health. Be sure you are getting 7 to 8 hours every night.

Here are some tips for getting the best night sleep:

  1. Get on a regular schedule.​Going to sleep and waking up at the same time each day instills a familiar rhythm.
  2. Use your bed for sleep and romance only.
  3. Make your bedroom a peaceful, quiet haven.
  4. Get at least 20 minutes of sunshine every day,​preferably in the morning. This triggers your brain to release chemicals that help regulate sleep cycles.
  5. Shut off bright screens before bed.​
  6. Try blue­light nights.​Blue­spectrum light helps your brain reset for sleep and increases melatonin. Try to put on only blue lights for three hours before bed. You can buy them at LowBlueLights.com
  7. Reset your nervous system​. Using an acupressure mat helps to increase your parasympathetic nervous system and get you into deep relaxation before sleep. Lie on it for 35 minutes before bed. You can find one at the Spoonk website (www.spoonkspace.com).
  8. Get grounded​. Sometimes electromagnetic frequencies (EMFs) impair sleep. Turn off Wi­Fi. Keep electronic devices (like your phone or radio) away from your bed. Try an “earthing sheet,” which grounds you to the earth and disconnects you from EMFs that can disrupt your sleep. Check out Earthing.com
  9. Clear your mind​. Keep a journal or notebook by your bed and write down all your to­dos or ruminations before you go to sleep so when you close your eyes your mind will be less likely to spin.
  10. Do some light stretching​or yoga before bedtime. Relax your body.
  11. Warm your core.​This raises your core temperature and triggers the proper chemistry for sleep. Use a hot water bottle or a heating pad.
  12. Use herbal therapies​. Try 300 to 600 milligrams of passionflower or 320 to 480 milligrams of valerian root extract before bed. You can purchase this on my store.
  13. Try other natural sleep supplements.​Try one at a time to see what works for you. Start with melatonin and magnesium, which are often enough for most people. Some of these come in combinations.
    • 1 to 3 milligrams of melatonin
    • 150 to 300 milligrams of magnesium
    • 200 to 400 milligrams of theanine
    • 500 to 1,000 milligrams of GABA
    • 50 to 200 milligrams of 5 hydroxytryptophan
    • 365 milligrams of magnolia
  14. Use relaxation practices before bedtime​to unwind. Examples include guided imagery, meditation, deep breathing, etc.

Now, let’s move on to relaxation. Now, we aren’t talking about watching TV all night long. We are talking about activities that recharge and restore you. These are activities like:

  • Doing yoga
  • Practicing meditation
  • Doing deep breathing exercises
  • Taking a steam or sauna
  • Enjoying a hot bath (with 2 cups of Epsom salt and 10 drops of lavender oil — what Dr. Hyman calls his Detox Bath)

It is so important during the Eat Fat, Get Thin 21­Day Plan to make time for self care. Along with redesigning your sleep, take some time this week to bring attention to the type of environment that you would like to design for yourself after the program is over. By setting up healthy cues in your home and in your social settings, you help yourself create healthy habits.
