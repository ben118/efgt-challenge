Overview

Welcome to the E​at Fat, Get Thin​21­Day Plan. We are so excited that you have taken this step towards better health! This plan is all about busting our fear of fat while following a plan that brings you greater mental clarity, weight loss, balanced energy, deeper sleep and overall better health. Before we start day one, there are a couple of steps that we recommend you take so that you are prepared to start your program.

Step 1: Establish Your Baseline: Take the Quizzes and Intake Survey

Before you embark on the E​at Fat, Get Thin​journey, it’s important to establish where you are starting from and prepare yourself for the 21­day trek by taking the F​LC and the Diabesity / Carbohydrate Intolerance Quizzes.​You will also retake these quizzes at the end of the program. We guarantee you will be amazed at the changes in just three short weeks.

We also would really appreciate if you could take our short intake survey. The information you provide will help us continue to improve the program and make sure we continue to help you achieve your goals.

Also, make sure to check with your doctor before you do this program.

Step 2: Kitchen Makeover

During this stage of the plan, take some time to detox your kitchen of the items in the list below. Don’t just tuck these away – toss them in the garbage! If you have a moment of panic, rest assured: we’re going to quickly and easily replace them with delicious, healthy alternatives that will leave you more than satisfied.

  • Anything that is not real food and processed foods.
  • All foods that contains sugar
  • Any foods with artificial sweeteners, preservatives, additives and coloring or dyes
  • Any food or drink that contains sugar or any kind
  • Anything that contains hydrogenated oils and refined vegetable oils

The following items also have to go, but if you feel uncomfortable throwing them away, just put them out of sight during the 21­day plan, as long as you feel you can safely avoid them.

  • All products containing gluten
  • All grains
  • All dairy products
  • All beans

We guarantee that if you make time to detox your kitchen before you start the plan, you will feel much more prepared to start and will feel ready for success beginning on day one.

Step 3: Stock Your Toolbox and Gather Your Supplies

We have created the E​at Fat, Get Thin​staples shopping list (found in the Companion Guide in the resources section) to help you with this process. You will need these essential items for the entire 21­days of your program.

Exercise Apparel

Now is the time to start thinking about scheduling exercise into your routine if you don’t already. The easier you make it for yourself to jump out the door and get moving, the more likely you are to do it regularly. Get your sneakers out of the closet, or go buy a new pair. Choose whatever clothing you’ll be most comfortable in for walking, and make sure it’s clean and accessible. By making sure you have the right attire ready, clean, and available, you’ll be set to lace up and exercise on day one.

Step 4: Take Your Measurements

Studies show you will achieve up to 50 percent more success if you keep track of your progress. Take the following measurements the day before you start the program and record them in your journal PDF. You can access your journal questions and measurements in the resources section.

  • Your weight ­ weigh yourself first thing in the morning without clothes and after going to the bathroom
  • Your height ­ measure your height in feet and inches
  • Your waist size ­ using a tape measure, find the widest point at your belly button, not where your belt falls
  • Your hip size ­ using a tape measure, find the widest point around your hips.
  • Your thigh circumference – using a tape measure, find the widest point around each thigh
  • Your blood pressure ­ this can be done by your doctor, at the drugstore, or at home if you purchase a basic home blood pressure cuff

Step 5: Get Tested by Your Doctor (optional)

The quizzes you take in Step 1 will give you a good overall sense of your health. However, there is a set of laboratory tests that I strongly encourage you to take that shows you what’s really going on. There are two levels of testing needed to properly assess diabesity and food addiction, its complications, and its causes. Take the tests before your detox and again after you have been on the Transition Phase for three months. By taking these tests, you will see how much the changes you’re making can transform your health.

Dr. Hyman created a very helpful e­book called​H​ow To Work With Your Doctor To Get What You Need​that you will find helpful during this testing process. You can access this e­book in the resources tab.

The tests he recommends include:

  • Insulin response test, which is like a two­hour glucose tolerance test but this one also measures insulin as well. It is done in three parts: first we measure your insulin and glucose first thing after fasting overnight. Then you consume a drink containing 75 grams of glucose. Then your insulin and glucose levels are again measured after one­ and two­hour intervals. This test is the best way to tell how your body processes glucose and produces insulin.
  • Hemoglobin A1c measures your average blood sugar over the past six weeks. Anything 5.5 percent or above is considered elevated; over 6.0 percent is means you have diabetes.
  • NMR lipid (cholesterol) profile or ,Cardio IQ Lipoprotein Fractionation test, which measures LDL, HDL, and triglycerides, and the particle number and particle size of each type of cholesterol and triglycerides. (This is a newer test, but I would demand it from your doctor, because the typical cholesterol tests done by most labs and doctors are out of date.) This test can only be obtained through LabCorp, Quest or LipoScience. You can find a link to these sites at​
    w​ww.EatFatGetThin.com/resources.​
  • Total cholesterol – ideally under 200 mg/dl but this matters less than the overall profile. If your total cholesterol is 300 mg/dl and your HDL is 100 mg/dl, then that is much better than a total cholesterol of 150 mg/dl and an HDL of 30 mg/dl.
  • LDL cholesterol – ideally under 100 mg/dl but more important is the total particle number which should be less than 1000 and the small LDL particle number which should be less than 400 (or even lower)
  • HDL cholesterol – ideally over 50 mg/dl for men and over 60 mg/dl for women
  • Triglycerides – okay if under 100 mg/dl; ideal if under 70 mg/dl
  • Triglycerides to HDL ration ­ ideally less than 1:1 or 2:1
  • Total cholesterol to HDL ratio ­ ideally less than 3:1
